SECTION 1: CNC MILLING MACHINE (CNC-M01) — CALIBRATION
=========================================================

1. STARTUP PROCEDURE VERIFICATION
─────────────────────────────────
Before running any production job, operators must complete:
Step 1: Power ON machine and wait for CNC controller boot (45 seconds).
Step 2: Press MACHINE HOME — all three axes (X, Y, Z) return to zero position.
Step 3: Load job-specific tool library from controller tool menu.
Step 4: Set workpiece zero (G54 offset) using edge finder or tool probe.
Step 5: Run DRY CYCLE with Feed Hold ON — verify tool paths are correct.
Step 6: Set spindle speed as per job card (range: 500–8000 RPM).
Step 7: Begin machining — monitor first pass closely for chatter or overload.
Startup verification must be signed off by shift supervisor for new jobs.

2. GEOMETRIC ACCURACY SPECIFICATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────
  X-axis straightness:     ≤ 0.010 mm per 300 mm travel
  Y-axis straightness:     ≤ 0.010 mm per 300 mm travel
  Z-axis straightness:     ≤ 0.008 mm per 150 mm travel
  X-Y squareness:          ≤ 0.015 mm per 300 mm
  Spindle runout (radial): ≤ 0.005 mm (measured at spindle nose)
  Spindle runout (axial):  ≤ 0.003 mm
  Backlash X:              ≤ 0.010 mm
  Backlash Y:              ≤ 0.010 mm
  Backlash Z:              ≤ 0.008 mm

If any parameter exceeds specification, machine must be re-calibrated
before production continues. Tag machine OUT OF SERVICE until cleared.

3. TOOL LENGTH CALIBRATION PROCEDURE
──────────────────────────────────────
Step 1: Load tool in spindle.
Step 2: Navigate to TOOL OFFSET page on controller.
Step 3: Move Z-axis until tool touches calibration block surface.
Step 4: Record Z position — enter as tool length offset in controller.
Step 5: Verify by comparing measured length to actual tool length.
Step 6: Tolerance: ±0.02 mm — re-measure if outside tolerance.
Step 7: Document in calibration log with tool number, length, date.

4. SPINDLE WARM-UP PROCEDURE
──────────────────────────────
Run spindle warm-up at start of shift or after idle > 4 hours:
Step 1: Run spindle at 1000 RPM for 3 minutes.
Step 2: Increase to 3000 RPM for 3 minutes.
Step 3: Increase to 5000 RPM for 3 minutes.
Step 4: Increase to maximum operating RPM for 2 minutes.
Step 5: Check spindle temperature — must not exceed 50°C after warm-up.
Warm-up prevents thermal expansion errors and extends bearing life.

5. COOLANT SYSTEM CALIBRATION
───────────────────────────────
  Target coolant concentration: 8–10% (check with refractometer)
  Coolant pH target:            8.5–9.5
  Maximum bacterial count:      < 10^4 CFU/mL (monthly lab test)
  Coolant flow at nozzle:       8–15 L/min
  Coolant temperature:          ≤ 30°C (coolant unit thermostat setting)

SECTION 2: HYDRAULIC PUMP UNIT (HYD-P02) — SPECIFICATIONS
=========================================================

1. SYSTEM OPERATING SPECIFICATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────
  Maximum system pressure:    250 bar (working)
  Relief valve set pressure:  280 bar
  Hydraulic fluid type:       Mineral oil ISO VG 46
  Reservoir capacity:         200 litres
  Pump type:                  Axial piston variable displacement
  Motor rating:               22 kW, 415V, 3-phase, 50 Hz
  Maximum fluid temperature:  60°C continuous, 70°C peak
  Filtration rating:          10 micron (return), 25 micron (suction)

2. PRESSURE CALIBRATION PROCEDURE
────────────────────────────────────
Step 1: Install calibrated pressure gauge at test point TP-01.
Step 2: Start pump and allow 5-minute warm-up at low pressure.
Step 3: Slowly increase pressure to system setpoint using pressure-reducing valve.
Step 4: Verify main gauge reading matches calibrated gauge ±2%.
Step 5: Test PRV: increase pressure until PRV lifts — record lift pressure.
        PRV must lift at 280 ± 5 bar.
Step 6: Document calibration date, gauge serial number, readings.
Calibration frequency: Quarterly, or after any PRV replacement.

3. FLUID ANALYSIS PARAMETERS
──────────────────────────────
Monthly oil sample must be tested for:
  Viscosity at 40°C:     44–48 cSt (ISO VG 46 spec)
  Water content:         < 0.1%
  Particle count:        ISO 4406 cleanliness ≤ 18/16/13
  TAN (Total Acid Number): < 2.0 mg KOH/g
  Metal content:         Fe < 20 ppm, Cu < 10 ppm
If any parameter is out of range: drain and replace fluid immediately.

SECTION 3: CONVEYOR BELT SYSTEM (CVB-003) — SPECIFICATIONS
=========================================================

1. BELT TRACKING ADJUSTMENT PROCEDURE
────────────────────────────────────────
Conveyor belt must track within ±10 mm of center at all times.
Step 1: Start conveyor at normal operating speed with no product load.
Step 2: Observe belt position at tail pulley for 2 minutes.
Step 3: If belt drifts LEFT: tighten RIGHT tracking bolt by 1/4 turn clockwise.
Step 4: If belt drifts RIGHT: tighten LEFT tracking bolt by 1/4 turn clockwise.
Step 5: Wait 30 seconds after each adjustment — observe new tracking position.
Step 6: Repeat until belt runs centered within ±10 mm.
Step 7: Maximum adjustment: 3 full turns from neutral (factory setting).
Step 8: If belt cannot be centered within 3 turns: stop conveyor and contact
        maintenance — frame may be out of square or belt may be damaged.
Caution: Never adjust tracking while belt is stationary — results will be incorrect.

2. BELT TENSION SPECIFICATION
───────────────────────────────
  Minimum belt tension:       800 N (below this: slip risk)
  Maximum belt tension:       1200 N (above this: bearing overload)
  Measurement method:         Tension meter at center span (mid-point)
  Measurement frequency:      Monthly (B-level PM)
  Adjustment method:          Tail pulley take-up bolts, equal tension both sides

3. EMERGENCY STOP PROCEDURE
─────────────────────────────
Conveyor has three methods of emergency stopping:
Method 1 — Pull Cord: Pull the red rope running along the conveyor side.
  Belt stops within 3 seconds. Pull cord must be manually reset by twisting.
  Test: Pull cord must be tested every shift — required safety check.
Method 2 — E-STOP Button: Press red mushroom head button on control panel.
  Belt stops immediately. Button must be twisted to release before restart.
Method 3 — HMI Stop: Touch STOP button on HMI screen.
  Belt decelerates over 5 seconds (controlled stop — preferred for product safety).
After any emergency stop: investigate cause before restarting.
All emergency stop activations must be logged in the shift report.

4. SPEED AND LOAD SPECIFICATIONS
──────────────────────────────────
  Belt speed range:         0.2 – 1.5 m/s (set per production order)
  Maximum belt load:        200 kg/m distributed load
  Maximum single item:      50 kg at 0.5 m spacing
  Drive motor rated power:  7.5 kW
  Gearbox ratio:            1:25
  Drive pulley diameter:    315 mm
