Document No: SAF-001

=========================================================
1. GENERAL PLANT SAFETY RULES
=========================================================
1.  All personnel must wear mandatory PPE at all times in production areas.
2.  No unauthorized personnel are permitted in restricted zones.
3.  All incidents, near misses, and unsafe conditions must be reported immediately.
4.  Mobile phones are prohibited in machining and welding areas.
5.  Food and drink are not permitted in production areas.
6.  Running is prohibited in the plant at all times.
7.  All spills must be cleaned up immediately — slip hazard.
8.  Overhead crane operation requires certification — no unauthorized use.
9.  Safety signs must be obeyed at all times.
10. Exits and fire points must remain unobstructed at all times.

=========================================================
2. PPE REQUIREMENTS BY AREA
=========================================================

AREA: Machining Cell (CNC Zone)
Required PPE:
  - Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1 rated) — MANDATORY
  - Steel-toed safety boots (S3 rated) — MANDATORY
  - Cut-resistant gloves (during tool handling only)
  - Hearing protection (> 85 dB areas — use sound level meter to check)
PROHIBITED PPE:
  - Loose gloves near rotating spindles (entanglement hazard)
  - Open-toe or canvas shoes
  - Loose clothing or dangling jewelry

AREA: Press & Hydraulic Cell
Required PPE:
  - Safety glasses — MANDATORY
  - Steel-toed boots — MANDATORY
  - Oil-resistant gloves
  - Face shield (when disconnecting hydraulic lines)
  - FR (flame-retardant) coveralls (near hot hydraulic lines)

AREA: Conveyor & Material Handling
Required PPE:
  - Safety glasses — MANDATORY
  - Steel-toed boots — MANDATORY
  - High-visibility (Hi-Vis) vest — MANDATORY
PROHIBITED PPE:
  - Gloves near moving conveyor (entanglement hazard)

AREA: Boiler Room
Required PPE:
  - Safety glasses — MANDATORY
  - Steel-toed boots — MANDATORY
  - Heat-resistant gloves — MANDATORY
  - Face shield (during blowdown operations)
  - FR coveralls — MANDATORY
  - Hearing protection (near burner)

AREA: Robotic Welding Cell
Required PPE:
  - Safety glasses — MANDATORY
  - Steel-toed boots — MANDATORY
  - Welding shield (auto-darkening, Shade 10+) during arc welding
  - FR gloves
  - Hi-Vis vest (outside cell fencing)
PROHIBITED:
  - No entry to cell during AUTO operation

3. LOCKOUT / TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE
=========================================================
LOTO must be applied before any maintenance, cleaning, or adjustment
on machinery. FAILURE TO FOLLOW LOTO IS A DISMISSIBLE OFFENCE.

STEP-BY-STEP LOTO PROCEDURE:
---------------------------------
Step 1: NOTIFY — Inform affected personnel and supervisor that machine is being locked out.
Step 2: IDENTIFY — Identify ALL energy sources (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, stored).
Step 3: SHUTDOWN — Turn off machine using normal stopping procedure.
Step 4: ISOLATE — Turn off and isolate each energy source:
         - Electrical: Turn isolator to OFF position.
         - Hydraulic: Close hydraulic supply valve, depressurize.
         - Pneumatic: Turn off air supply, exhaust residual pressure.
         - Stored energy: Lower suspended loads, release compressed springs.
Step 5: LOCK — Apply personal padlock to each isolation point.
         Rule: ONE LOCK PER PERSON working on the machine.
Step 6: TAG — Attach RED "DANGER — DO NOT OPERATE" tag to each lock.
Step 7: VERIFY — Test that machine CANNOT start:
         - Electrical: Check with multimeter — verify zero voltage.
         - Hydraulic: Verify gauge reads zero pressure.
         - Pneumatic: Open test valve — verify no airflow.
Step 8: WORK — Proceed with maintenance task.
Step 9: RESTORE — Remove all tools and personnel from machine.
Step 10: REMOVE LOCK — Each person removes their OWN lock only.
Step 11: NOTIFY — Inform operators and supervisor that machine is restored.

CRITICAL LOTO RULES:
  - NEVER remove someone else's lock.
  - NEVER work on a machine without your own lock applied.
  - If lock key is lost: call supervisor — special removal procedure applies.
  - ALL energy sources must be isolated — not just electrical.

4. HAZARD IDENTIFICATION & RISK MATRIX
=========================================================

RISK LEVEL    | PROBABILITY x SEVERITY | ACTION REQUIRED
--------------|------------------------|------------------------------------------
EXTREME       | High x High           | Stop work immediately. Escalate to HSE.
HIGH          | High x Medium         | Correct before restarting. Notify supervisor.
MEDIUM        | Low x High            | Schedule correction within current shift.
LOW           | Low x Low             | Monitor. Log in hazard register.

5. CHEMICAL HAZARDS IN PLANT
=========================================================
Chemical                  | Location          | Hazard          | PPE Required
--------------------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------------
Coolant Concentrate       | Machining Cell    | Skin irritant   | Nitrile gloves, glasses
Hydraulic Oil ISO VG 46   | Hydraulic Cell    | Slip hazard     | Oil gloves, boots
Anti-Scale Chemical       | Boiler Room       | Corrosive       | Gloves, face shield, apron
Oxygen Scavenger          | Boiler Room       | Irritant        | Gloves, glasses
Gear Oil ISO VG 220       | Conveyor Area     | Slip hazard     | Oil gloves
Welding Fumes             | Robotic Cell      | Respiratory     | RPE if ventilation fails
Cutting Fluids            | Machining Cell    | Skin sensitizer | Nitrile gloves

All chemicals must have SDS (Safety Data Sheets) available at point of use.

6. FIRE SAFETY
=========================================================
Fire Extinguisher Types:
  - CO2 (Black): Electrical fires, hydraulic fluid fires — DO NOT use on boiler
  - Dry Powder (Blue): General, flammable liquid
  - Water (Red): Paper, wood only — NEVER on electrical or flammable liquid

Fire Evacuation Procedure:
  1. Raise alarm — press nearest break-glass unit.
  2. Shut down machines using E-STOP if safe to do so.
  3. Evacuate via nearest marked EXIT — do not use elevators.
  4. Assemble at MUSTER POINT (Car Park A, West Gate).
  5. Report to Area Warden — confirm headcount.
  6. Do NOT re-enter building until ALL CLEAR from Fire Marshal.

8. INCIDENT REPORTING PROCEDURE
=========================================================
ALL of the following must be reported immediately:
  - Any injury (however minor)
  - Near misses
  - Equipment damage
  - Dangerous conditions
  - Environmental incidents (spills reaching drains)

Reporting Steps:
  1. Ensure area is safe — provide first aid if needed.
  2. Notify shift supervisor immediately.
  3. Complete INCIDENT REPORT FORM (Form HSE-IR-001).
  4. Do not disturb scene until investigation is complete.
  5. Serious injuries: notify HSE department within 1 hour.

=========================================================
9. PERMIT TO WORK SYSTEM
=========================================================
The following work ALWAYS requires a Permit to Work (PTW):
  - Hot work (welding, grinding near flammables)
  - Confined space entry
  - Work at height (> 1.8 meters)
  - Electrical work on live systems
  - Hydraulic system work under pressure
  - Boiler internal inspection

PTW forms must be approved by the Area Supervisor and HSE before work begins.

=========================================================
10. SAFETY SIGN MEANINGS
=========================================================
RED CIRCLE (Prohibition): Action is FORBIDDEN.
  Example: No smoking. No entry.

YELLOW TRIANGLE (Warning): Hazard nearby — take care.
  Example: Forklift area. High voltage. Hot surface.

BLUE CIRCLE (Mandatory): Action MUST be done.
  Example: Wear safety glasses. Wear gloves.

GREEN SQUARE (Safe Condition): Safety information.
  Example: First aid. Emergency exit. Assembly point.
