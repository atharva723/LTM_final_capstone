STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Machine: Conveyor Belt System
Machine ID: CVB-003
Document No: SOP-CVB-003
Version: 1.2
Effective Date: 2024-01-20
Department: Material Handling & Assembly

=========================================================
1. PURPOSE
=========================================================
This SOP governs the safe startup, operation, and shutdown of the main assembly
conveyor belt (CVB-003) used to transport components between workstations.

=========================================================
2. REQUIRED PPE
=========================================================
- Safety glasses
- Steel-toed boots
- High-visibility vest (mandatory near conveyor)
- Gloves prohibited near moving belt/rollers (entanglement hazard)

=========================================================
3. PRE-STARTUP CHECKLIST
=========================================================
Step 1: Walk the entire conveyor length — check for objects on belt.
Step 2: Verify all emergency pull-cord stops are unobstructed and functional.
Step 3: Check belt tension — must not sag > 20mm at center span.
Step 4: Inspect belt surface for cuts, fraying, or tracking issues.
Step 5: Check all roller bearings for noise or stiffness.
Step 6: Confirm all guards and covers are in place.
Step 7: Verify drive gearbox oil level.
Step 8: Ensure no personnel are in the conveyor path.

=========================================================
4. STARTUP PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Sound alarm horn (3-second warning) before starting.
Step 2: Turn control panel key switch to ON.
Step 3: Press START button — belt accelerates to set speed over 5 seconds.
Step 4: Observe belt tracking for first 2 minutes — must run centered.
Step 5: Adjust tracking if belt drifts > 10mm to either side.
Step 6: Verify conveyor speed on HMI (set per production order).

=========================================================
5. MONITORING PARAMETERS
=========================================================
| Parameter          | Normal Range     | Warning          | Alarm            |
|--------------------|------------------|------------------|------------------|
| Belt Speed         | 0.2 – 1.5 m/s    | > 1.6 m/s        | > 1.8 m/s        |
| Motor Temperature  | 20 – 70 °C       | > 75 °C          | > 85 °C          |
| Motor Current      | 5 – 25 A         | > 28 A           | > 35 A           |
| Belt Tension       | 800 – 1200 N     | < 600 N          | < 400 N          |
| Vibration (drive)  | 0.3 – 2.0 mm/s  | > 3.0 mm/s       | > 5.0 mm/s       |
| Roller Temp        | Ambient to 50 °C | > 55 °C          | > 65 °C          |

=========================================================
6. BELT TRACKING ADJUSTMENT
=========================================================
If belt drifts to LEFT:
  - Tighten RIGHT tracking bolt (tail pulley) by 1/4 turn.
  - Wait 30 seconds and observe. Repeat if needed.

If belt drifts to RIGHT:
  - Tighten LEFT tracking bolt (tail pulley) by 1/4 turn.
  - Maximum adjustment: 3 full turns from neutral position.
  - If tracking cannot be corrected: stop belt, call maintenance.

=========================================================
7. SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Clear all products from conveyor belt.
Step 2: Press STOP button — belt decelerates and stops.
Step 3: Turn key switch to OFF.
Step 4: Apply lockout to main control panel.
Step 5: Clean belt surface and remove any spillage.
Step 6: Record runtime hours in logbook.

=========================================================
8. EMERGENCY STOP PROCEDURES
=========================================================
- Pull Cord: Pull red cord along conveyor side — immediate stop.
- E-STOP Button: Press red mushroom button on control panel.
- After E-STOP: Do not restart until cause is identified and cleared.
- Entanglement: Do NOT try to pull free. Hit E-STOP. Call emergency services.

=========================================================
9. COMMON ERROR CODES
=========================================================
E21 — Belt Slip: Belt slipping on drive pulley. Check tension and loading.
E22 — Motor Overload: Overloaded belt. Reduce product load or speed.
E23 — Belt Misalignment: Belt tracking off. Stop and adjust tail pulley.
E24 — Roller Jam: Debris in roller. Clear belt and inspect rollers.
E25 — Drive Gearbox Fault: Oil low or gearbox overheating. Stop and inspect.
