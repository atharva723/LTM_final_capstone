STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Machine: Industrial Steam Boiler
Machine ID: BLR-004
Document No: SOP-BLR-004
Version: 3.0
Effective Date: 2024-03-01
Department: Utilities & Steam Generation
Regulatory Ref: IBR 1950, IS 2825

=========================================================
1. PURPOSE
=========================================================
This SOP defines procedures for the safe operation of the Industrial Steam Boiler
(BLR-004). Improper boiler operation is a life-safety hazard. Only certified
boiler operators may operate this equipment.

=========================================================
2. REQUIRED PPE
=========================================================
- Heat-resistant gloves (mandatory)
- Face shield
- Fire-retardant coveralls
- Safety boots
- Hearing protection near burner area

=========================================================
3. PRE-STARTUP CHECKLIST
=========================================================
Step 1: Verify water level in sight glass — must be between L and H marks.
Step 2: Check chemical dosing tank levels (anti-scale, oxygen scavenger).
Step 3: Inspect all safety valves — must move freely (manual lift test).
Step 4: Check fuel supply pressure (gas: 100–200 mbar).
Step 5: Verify all drain and blowdown valves are closed.
Step 6: Confirm stack damper is open.
Step 7: Check burner igniter electrode condition.
Step 8: Verify steam pressure gauge reads zero (cold start).
Step 9: Sign pre-startup checklist and submit to shift supervisor.

=========================================================
4. STARTUP PROCEDURE (COLD START)
=========================================================
Step 1: Fill boiler to normal water level using feedwater pump.
Step 2: Open air vent valve to purge air from steam space.
Step 3: Start forced draft (FD) fan — purge furnace for 5 minutes.
Step 4: Ignite pilot burner.
Step 5: Open main fuel valve slowly — confirm flame on CCTV.
Step 6: Raise steam pressure gradually (max 1 bar per 5 minutes).
Step 7: Close air vent when steam begins flowing (approx. 0.5 bar).
Step 8: Bring boiler to operating pressure: 8–10 bar (approx. 45–60 min).
Step 9: Open steam supply valve to process gradually.

=========================================================
5. MONITORING PARAMETERS
=========================================================
| Parameter             | Normal Range      | Warning           | Alarm / Trip      |
|-----------------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Steam Pressure        | 7 – 10 bar        | > 10.5 bar        | > 11 bar (trip)   |
| Water Level           | 50 – 70% gauge    | < 30% or > 85%    | < 15% (trip)      |
| Flue Gas Temp         | 180 – 240 °C      | > 260 °C          | > 300 °C          |
| Fuel Pressure (Gas)   | 100 – 200 mbar    | < 80 mbar         | < 60 mbar (trip)  |
| Feedwater Temp        | 80 – 105 °C       | < 70 °C           | < 60 °C           |
| O2 in Flue Gas        | 2 – 5 %           | < 1% or > 8%      | < 0.5%            |
| Flame Signal          | Strong (green)    | Weak (amber)      | No flame (trip)   |

=========================================================
6. BLOWDOWN PROCEDURE (Daily)
=========================================================
WARNING: Wear full PPE. Blowdown involves high-pressure steam and water.
Step 1: Ensure water level is at HIGH mark before blowdown.
Step 2: Open bottom blowdown valve slowly (surface blowdown first).
Step 3: Hold open for 3–5 seconds only.
Step 4: Close valve firmly.
Step 5: Check water level — refill if needed via feedwater pump.
Step 6: Log time and duration of blowdown in logbook.

=========================================================
7. SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Close main steam supply valve.
Step 2: Reduce fuel input to minimum.
Step 3: Shut off fuel supply — flame extinguishes.
Step 4: Run FD fan for 5 minutes post-shutdown (furnace purge).
Step 5: Allow pressure to drop naturally — do not force cool.
Step 6: Maintain water level above LOW mark during cooldown.
Step 7: Close all valves when pressure reaches 0 bar.
Step 8: Log shutdown time.

=========================================================
8. SAFETY TRIPS (AUTO-SHUTDOWN)
=========================================================
The boiler will AUTOMATICALLY SHUTDOWN (lockout) if:
- Steam pressure > 11 bar (safety valve lifts + burner trips)
- Water level < 15% (low-low water trip)
- Flame failure (no flame signal for > 5 seconds)
- Fuel pressure < 60 mbar
- FD fan failure

After a safety trip: DO NOT restart without identifying and clearing the cause.
Log the trip in the incident register and notify the boiler inspector.

=========================================================
9. COMMON ERROR CODES
=========================================================
E31 — Low Water Level: Feed water immediately. Check feedwater pump.
E32 — High Steam Pressure: Reduce firing rate. Check demand valve.
E33 — Flame Failure: Check fuel supply, igniter, and flame detector.
E34 — FD Fan Fault: Check fan motor and impeller. Do not fire without FD fan.
E35 — High Flue Gas Temp: Check for scale buildup in tubes. Schedule tube cleaning.
