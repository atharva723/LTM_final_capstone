STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Machine: CNC Milling Machine
Machine ID: CNC-M01
Document No: SOP-CNC-001
Version: 2.1
Effective Date: 2024-01-15
Department: Machining Cell A

=========================================================
1. PURPOSE
=========================================================
This SOP defines the standard operating procedure for the CNC Milling Machine (CNC-M01)
to ensure safe, consistent, and high-quality machining operations.

=========================================================
2. SCOPE
=========================================================
Applies to all operators, maintenance personnel, and supervisors working with CNC-M01
in Machining Cell A.

=========================================================
3. RESPONSIBILITIES
=========================================================
- Operator: Follow startup, operation, and shutdown procedures.
- Shift Supervisor: Verify machine readiness and sign off on job setup.
- Maintenance Engineer: Perform scheduled PM and respond to fault alerts.

=========================================================
4. REQUIRED PPE
=========================================================
- Safety glasses (mandatory at all times)
- Steel-toed boots
- Cut-resistant gloves (during tool changes only)
- Hearing protection (during continuous cutting operations > 30 min)
- NO loose clothing, jewelry, or gloves during active spindle rotation

=========================================================
5. PRE-STARTUP CHECKLIST
=========================================================
Step 1: Inspect machine for visible damage, loose guards, or debris.
Step 2: Check coolant level — must be above MIN line on reservoir.
Step 3: Verify spindle oil level in the lubrication sight glass.
Step 4: Check that all axis limit switches are unobstructed.
Step 5: Ensure emergency stop (E-STOP) button is functional — press and release test.
Step 6: Confirm tool holders are clean and tool offsets are loaded.
Step 7: Check workpiece clamping — torque must be within spec per job card.
Step 8: Verify the correct G-code program is loaded and reviewed.

=========================================================
6. STARTUP PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Turn main power switch ON (located on rear panel).
Step 2: Wait for CNC controller to boot — approx. 45 seconds.
Step 3: Press "MACHINE HOME" button — all axes will return to zero.
Step 4: Load the job-specific tool library from the controller menu.
Step 5: Set workpiece zero (G54 offset) using edge finder or probe.
Step 6: Run a dry cycle (Feed Hold ON) to verify tool paths.
Step 7: Set spindle speed per job card (typical range: 500–8000 RPM).
Step 8: Begin machining — monitor first pass closely.

=========================================================
7. DURING OPERATION — MONITORING PARAMETERS
=========================================================
| Parameter         | Normal Range       | Warning Threshold  | Alarm Threshold   |
|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Spindle RPM       | 500 – 8000 RPM     | > 8200 RPM         | > 8500 RPM        |
| Spindle Temp      | 20 – 65 °C         | > 70 °C            | > 80 °C           |
| Vibration         | 0.5 – 2.5 mm/s    | > 3.5 mm/s         | > 5.0 mm/s        |
| Feed Rate         | Per G-code         | ±10% deviation     | ±20% deviation    |
| Coolant Flow      | 8 – 15 L/min       | < 6 L/min          | < 3 L/min         |
| Power Consumption | 2 – 15 kW          | > 18 kW            | > 22 kW           |

=========================================================
8. SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Complete current machining cycle — do not interrupt mid-pass.
Step 2: Return all axes to home position.
Step 3: Turn off spindle and coolant pump.
Step 4: Remove workpiece and clean fixture.
Step 5: Clean machine bed — remove chips using brush or chip conveyor.
Step 6: Apply rust-preventive oil on exposed guideways.
Step 7: Power off CNC controller.
Step 8: Turn main power switch OFF.
Step 9: Complete job log and update machine logbook.

=========================================================
9. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
=========================================================
- Tool Breakage: Press E-STOP immediately. Do not reach inside machine.
- Fire: Press E-STOP, activate fire extinguisher (CO2 type only), evacuate, call supervisor.
- Coolant Leak: Press E-STOP, isolate power, contain spill with absorbent.
- Operator Injury: Press E-STOP, call first aid, do not move injured person.

=========================================================
10. COMMON ERROR CODES — QUICK REFERENCE
=========================================================
E01 — Spindle Overload: Reduce feed rate, inspect tool.
E02 — Axis Drive Fault: Check servo drive panel, reset and home.
E03 — Coolant Low: Refill coolant tank immediately.
E04 — Door Interlock Open: Close machine door fully.
E05 — Tool Length Error: Re-measure and update tool offset.
