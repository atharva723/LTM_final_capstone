STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Machine: Hydraulic Pump Unit
Machine ID: HYD-P02
Document No: SOP-HYD-002
Version: 1.4
Effective Date: 2024-02-01
Department: Press & Forming Cell

=========================================================
1. PURPOSE
=========================================================
This SOP defines safe operating procedures for the Hydraulic Pump Unit (HYD-P02)
used in press and forming operations.

=========================================================
2. REQUIRED PPE
=========================================================
- Safety glasses
- Oil-resistant gloves
- Steel-toed boots
- Face shield (when breaking hydraulic connections)
- Flame-retardant coveralls when working near hot hydraulic lines

=========================================================
3. PRE-STARTUP CHECKLIST
=========================================================
Step 1: Check hydraulic fluid level — must be between MIN and MAX on sight glass.
Step 2: Inspect all hydraulic hoses for cracks, bulges, or abrasion marks.
Step 3: Check for fluid leaks around pump, valves, and cylinder connections.
Step 4: Verify pressure relief valve is set to correct pressure (per job card).
Step 5: Confirm filter indicator — replace filter if indicator is in RED zone.
Step 6: Check motor coupling for wear or looseness.
Step 7: Verify all hydraulic cylinder pins are secured.

=========================================================
4. STARTUP PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Turn main isolator switch ON.
Step 2: Start motor at low pressure (unloaded) — allow 2-minute warm-up.
Step 3: Gradually increase system pressure to operating level.
Step 4: Check for abnormal noise (knocking or whining = air in system).
Step 5: Verify actuator movement is smooth in both directions.
Step 6: Confirm pressure gauge reads within operating range.

=========================================================
5. MONITORING PARAMETERS
=========================================================
| Parameter            | Normal Range     | Warning           | Alarm             |
|----------------------|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| System Pressure      | 100 – 250 bar    | > 260 bar         | > 280 bar         |
| Fluid Temperature    | 35 – 60 °C       | > 65 °C           | > 75 °C           |
| Motor Current        | 10 – 40 A        | > 45 A            | > 55 A            |
| Flow Rate            | 20 – 80 L/min    | < 15 L/min        | < 8 L/min         |
| Vibration (pump)     | 0.5 – 3.0 mm/s  | > 4.0 mm/s        | > 6.0 mm/s        |
| Filter Differential  | < 3.5 bar        | > 3.5 bar         | > 5.0 bar         |

=========================================================
6. SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Reduce system pressure to zero (open unloading valve).
Step 2: Retract all cylinders to home position.
Step 3: Stop motor.
Step 4: Turn isolator switch OFF and lock out (LOTO).
Step 5: Allow system to cool before performing any maintenance.
Step 6: Log hours of operation in maintenance logbook.

=========================================================
7. LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE
=========================================================
WARNING: NEVER work on hydraulic system under pressure.
Step 1: Shut down pump motor.
Step 2: Turn isolator switch to OFF and apply personal lock.
Step 3: Attach RED "DO NOT OPERATE" tag to isolator.
Step 4: Release stored pressure using manual unloading valve.
Step 5: Verify zero pressure on gauge before proceeding.
Step 6: Place safety blocks under any suspended cylinder loads.

=========================================================
8. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
=========================================================
- High Pressure Leak (pinhole): EVACUATE immediately — fluid injection injuries are fatal.
  Do not touch the leak. Depressurize remotely. Call maintenance supervisor.
- Fluid Fire: CO2 or dry powder extinguisher only — never water.
- Motor Overload Trip: Check for blocked cylinder, overpressure, or overloaded circuit.

=========================================================
9. COMMON ERROR CODES
=========================================================
E11 — Overpressure: System pressure exceeded limit. Check relief valve.
E12 — Low Fluid Level: Refill reservoir with approved fluid (ISO VG 46).
E13 — High Fluid Temperature: Check cooler, increase cooling flow.
E14 — Filter Blocked: Replace hydraulic filter element.
E15 — Motor Overload: Check actuator for mechanical jam.
