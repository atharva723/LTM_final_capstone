STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Machine: 6-Axis Industrial Robotic Arm
Machine ID: ROB-005
Document No: SOP-ROB-005
Version: 2.0
Effective Date: 2024-02-15
Department: Welding & Assembly Automation

=========================================================
1. PURPOSE
=========================================================
This SOP defines operating procedures for the 6-Axis Robotic Arm (ROB-005) used
in automated welding and pick-and-place operations.

=========================================================
2. SCOPE
=========================================================
Applies to robot operators, programmers, and maintenance personnel.
This robot operates within a safety-fenced cell. NO entry during automatic operation.

=========================================================
3. REQUIRED PPE
=========================================================
- Safety glasses
- Steel-toed boots
- Welding shield (when robot is welding)
- Flame-retardant gloves (welding cell)
- High-visibility vest (outside robot cell)

=========================================================
4. SAFETY ZONES
=========================================================
Zone 1 (RED): Inside robot cell fencing — PROHIBITED during auto mode.
Zone 2 (YELLOW): 1 meter outside fencing — caution, stay alert.
Zone 3 (GREEN): Beyond 2 meters — safe observation zone.

Robot speed in MANUAL (Teach) mode is limited to 250 mm/s for safety.
Robot in AUTO mode: full speed up to 2000 mm/s — STAY CLEAR.

=========================================================
5. PRE-STARTUP CHECKLIST
=========================================================
Step 1: Inspect robot arm for physical damage or loose fasteners.
Step 2: Check all cable conduits and connectors — no fraying or kinks.
Step 3: Verify teach pendant is stored in holder (not in cell).
Step 4: Confirm safety light curtain and door interlock are functional.
Step 5: Check welding wire spool level and condition (if applicable).
Step 6: Inspect end-effector (gripper/welding torch) for wear.
Step 7: Verify compressed air supply (for pneumatic gripper): 5–6 bar.
Step 8: Confirm robot controller is powered and displays home screen.

=========================================================
6. STARTUP PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Turn main controller power ON.
Step 2: Wait for controller boot sequence to complete (~60 seconds).
Step 3: Press MOTORS ON button on teach pendant.
Step 4: Select AUTO mode on mode selector.
Step 5: Load correct job program from program library.
Step 6: Confirm safety cell gate is closed and interlocked.
Step 7: Press CYCLE START from outside the cell.
Step 8: Observe first cycle — verify motion paths and end-effector action.

=========================================================
7. MONITORING PARAMETERS
=========================================================
| Parameter              | Normal Range       | Warning           | Alarm              |
|------------------------|--------------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Joint Temperature      | 25 – 60 °C         | > 65 °C           | > 75 °C            |
| Motor Current (J1-J6)  | 2 – 18 A           | > 20 A            | > 25 A             |
| Payload                | 0 – 20 kg          | > 22 kg           | > 25 kg            |
| TCP Speed              | 0 – 2000 mm/s      | > 1900 mm/s       | > 2100 mm/s        |
| Air Pressure (gripper) | 5 – 6 bar          | < 4.5 bar         | < 4 bar            |
| Teach Pendant Battery  | > 30%              | 20–30%            | < 20%              |
| Vibration (base)       | 0.1 – 1.5 mm/s    | > 2.0 mm/s        | > 3.5 mm/s         |

=========================================================
8. TEACH/PROGRAMMING MODE
=========================================================
Step 1: Select MANUAL mode on mode selector — speed auto-limited to 250 mm/s.
Step 2: Enable deadman switch on teach pendant.
Step 3: Jog robot joints using pendant joystick.
Step 4: Record positions using RECORD POINT function.
Step 5: Test recorded path at low speed (10%) before setting full speed.
Step 6: Save program with unique job number and date.
Step 7: Return to AUTO mode when programming is complete.

=========================================================
9. SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
=========================================================
Step 1: Allow current cycle to complete.
Step 2: Press CYCLE STOP (not E-STOP unless emergency).
Step 3: Move robot to HOME/PARK position.
Step 4: Press MOTORS OFF.
Step 5: Power down controller.
Step 6: Turn main power isolator OFF.
Step 7: Lock control panel.

=========================================================
10. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
=========================================================
- Unexpected motion: Press E-STOP immediately (red button on all sides of cell).
- Person inside cell during operation: E-STOP, call supervisor, do not re-enter.
- Collision with fixture: Stop, inspect for damage, re-calibrate TCP before resuming.
- Fire: E-STOP, evacuate cell, CO2 extinguisher only.

=========================================================
11. COMMON ERROR CODES
=========================================================
E41 — Joint Overload: Payload exceeded. Check program and reduce load.
E42 — Motor Drive Fault: Check servo drive unit in controller cabinet.
E43 — Safety Circuit Open: Check cell gate interlock and light curtain.
E44 — Air Pressure Low: Check compressor and regulator. Inspect for leaks.
E45 — Program Not Found: Load correct job program from controller memory.
